Part 1: Key Business Concepts Explained Simply

What is a "System Outage"?

In the simplest words, a system outage is when a digital service or app stops working and users can't use it. Think of it like a power outage in your house. When the power is on, you can turn on lights and watch TV (the system is running). When the power goes out, none of those things work. If Netflix won't load movies or your bank app won't let you log in, that is a system outage.

What is a "Cascading Failure"?

A cascading failure is like a Domino Effect. It’s when one small thing breaks, and because that one thing broke, it causes the next thing to break, until the whole system crashes. Or think of a busy highway: if one car breaks down in the middle lane, everyone else slams on their brakes, and soon the entire highway is in a massive traffic jam. In software, if one piece gets too slow, the pieces waiting for it get stuck too, crashing the whole app.

What is an "SLA Violation"?

SLA stands for Service Level Agreement. It is a strict promise you make to customers about how well your system will work.

The Pizza Delivery Analogy: If a pizza place promises "30 minutes or it's free!", that promise is their SLA. If the pizza arrives in 35 minutes, they broke their promise. That is an SLA Violation, and the penalty is a free pizza.

In Software: If we promise a corporate client our app will load in under 2 seconds, and it suddenly takes 10 seconds, we broke the promise. The penalty is usually our company paying thousands of dollars in refunds or fines.

What is "Blockchain Auditing"?

Think of a normal company database like writing in a notebook with a pencil. If an engineer makes a mistake that crashes the system, they could erase the logs to save their job.

Blockchain is like writing in a notebook with permanent ink, and then handing out copies to thousands of people. Once you write something down on the blockchain, it can NEVER be erased or edited—not even by the CEO. It is the ultimate, undeniable proof to auditors that your company is being 100% honest.

What is "Telemetry"?

Think about driving a car. You have tiny sensors hidden in the engine measuring your speed and fuel level. They send that data to your dashboard so you know the health of the car. Telemetry is the software version of those car sensors. Apps automatically collect health data ("How many errors did I just have?") and send it back to DRCP so we can monitor the engine.

Who is "Gartner"? (From Slide 3)

Gartner is a very famous and highly respected technology research company. When pitching to a Director, they want to know your numbers are real. Using Gartner's name proves that the "$5,600 per minute" cost of downtime is a highly researched, industry-standard fact. It adds instant authority to your pitch.

"Let’s look at the actual problem. Today, we run our apps using hundreds of tiny connected pieces. If Service 1 goes down, Service 2 gets overloaded waiting for it and crashes too.
